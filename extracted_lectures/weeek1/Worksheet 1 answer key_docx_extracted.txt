Reasoning:
Technical Skills: These skills are specific to certain tools, technologies, or specialized knowledge required for particular job roles. For example, Microsoft Excel proficiency and Java programming are technical skills needed in roles like data analysis and software development.
Soft Skills: These skills are essential for effective communication, collaboration, and overall interpersonal interactions. For instance, communication, teamwork, and conflict resolution are essential in various workplace settings to build positive relationships and handle challenges.
Transferable Skills: These skills are versatile and can be applied in different work settings and roles. Problem-solving and critical thinking, for instance, are valuable skills across industries, enabling individuals to tackle complex challenges and find innovative solutions.
Please note that the categorization and reasoning may vary based on the groups' interpretations and understanding of the workplace skills. The goal of the activity is to encourage critical thinking and discussions among students regarding the significance of each skill in the workplace.
